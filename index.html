What "Extraction/Accuracy" measures

It grades how reliably a model pulls defined fields out of text that contains all the information but none of the structure — run-on notes, shorthand, inconsistent labels, facts stated once in passing. It rewards schema discipline and careful reading rather than raw intelligence, and the two do not track each other: on FormationEval's 72-model leaderboard, GLM-4.7 at $0.40/$1.50 placed 2nd and Kimi K2 Thinking placed 7th, both above Claude Opus 4.5 at twelve times the price.

Excellent Reliable schema adherence, strong grounding, handles inference from context. Great Solid extraction with a known weakness or thinner independent evidence. Good Workable, but likely to drop fields that require inference. Fair Control floor only — expect dropped sub-fields and silent failures.

Where these grades come from

Tiers — ExtractBench (Ferguson et al. 2026; 35 documents, 12,867 fields; failure-mode taxonomy), FormationEval (72-model leaderboard with cost columns), LLMStructBench (Tenckhoff et al. 2026; 22 models, five prompting strategies), JSONSchemaBench (Geng et al. 2025) and the Structured Output Benchmark. Full analysis in aiAssessmentCapability.md.
Prices and availability — verified 18 July 2026 against each provider's own pricing and model documentation, not third-party aggregators. See openaiModels.md for the OpenAI audit.
The caveat that matters — none of these benchmarks tests this task on these documents. They measure extraction from PDFs, research papers and safety sheets; the transfer to arborist notes is an assumption, not a result. Every tier is a hypothesis your own runs will confirm or overturn, which is what this bench is for.
